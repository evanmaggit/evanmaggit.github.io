Pacific Rhododendron (Rhododendron macrophyllum)

Common Alternate Names

1. Coast Rhododendron

2. Bigleaf Rhododendron

Physical Size

The Pacific Rhododendron can grow as a shrub around 3 to 6 feet tall.

It can also grow into a small tree reaching up to about 26 feet (8 meters) tall in favorable conditions.

Geographic Range

This plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, specifically from Northern California through Oregon and Washington.

Extending into British Columbia, Canada, and it is especially common in the Cascade Range and Olympic Mountains.

Climate Preference

The Pacific Rhododendron thrives in temperate maritime climates (cool summers, mild wet winters).

They prefer moist, acidic soils in coastal forests, foothills, and mountain slopes.
