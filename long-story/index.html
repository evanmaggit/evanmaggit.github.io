The house exhaled a slow, rotting breath each time Mara stepped across its warped floorboards.

She kept hearing a voice whisper her name from the walls, soft at first, then impatient, as if annoyed she hadn’t answered.

When she finally pressed her ear to the plaster, something warm pressed back from the other side.

By the time she realized it was a mouth, the whisper had already slipped inside her head and locked the door behind it.
